Independent science for understanding complex biological and cognitive systems.

We study neural systems, epidemics, and collective behaviour using mathematical modelling, data analysis, and network science. Our goal is to extract meaning from large-scale biological data and translate it into interpretable knowledge for science and society.

Our Approach

Modern science generates enormous amounts of biological data, but interpretation lags behind measurement. We develop mathematical and computational methods to infer structure, function, and dynamics from complex systems such as neural populations and spreading processes.

Research Areas

Neural Dynamics

Inference of neurons as dynamical systems.

Epidemic Modelling

Network and dynamical systems modelling of disease spread and intervention strategies.

Complex Systems

Emergence, collective behaviour, and dynamical processes on networks.
